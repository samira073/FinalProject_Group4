LOCATIONS

Willis Tower | Skydeck


Located at 233 South Wacker Drive, the 108 story office tower stands as the tallest building in the city. Its roofline reaches 1,451 FT / 442 M high while the antennas propel it to 1,729 FT / 527 M. Designed by Skidmore, Owings & Merrill LLP, the building was the tallest building in the world when it opened in 1974. Although it is now the 23rd tallest building in the world, The Skydeck remains the tallest observatory in the country, beating out the One World Trade Center in New York City. The observatory features glass extrusions that allow you to marvel down at the unobstructed view of the streets below.


ESTIMATED PRICE: $44 (adults); $22 (children above age 3)

Art Institute of Chicago | Museum


The Art Institute of Chicago was founded in 1879. The musuem features thousands of both famous and modern artworks from around the world. Some of its most famous pieces are Nighthawks, A Sunday on La Grande Jatte, and American Gothic. The musuem is conveniently only a few minutes south of Millennium Park. Its address is 111 South Michigan Avenue.


ESTIMATED PRICE: $14 base-ticket + exhibit addons

Shedd Aquarium | Museum


The Shedd Aquarium dates back to its opening on May 30, 1930. Located at 1200 S DuSable Lake Shore Drive, the aquarium is placed along the lakefront. It houses several exhibits featuring species from around the world including Beluga Whales, Sea Turtles, Leopard Sharks, Penguins, and Octopuses. It is currently the seventh largest aquarium in the world. The aquarium will only get bigger, as there is a planned renovation that is expected to be completed by the 100th anniversary of the musuem.


ESTIMATED PRICE: $19.95 Adult General Admission

Lincoln Park Zoo | Museum


The Lincoln Park Zoo is a zoo within a park, Lincoln Park. It was founded back in 1868. The 35-acre zoo is embedded within the park, providing a uniquely welcoming and open feel. The zoo features over 200 species, and over 1,100 animals. Despite being one of the best zoos in the country, it is entirely free!


ESTIMATED PRICE: FREE

Millennium Park | Public Space


Located at 201 E Randolph Street, Millennium Park is a relatively new addition to the city. The 24.5 acre park opened in 2004. In addition to vast green spaces, the park contains iconic fixtures such as the Cloud Gate (AKA "The Bean"), Jay Pritzker Pavilion, Crown Fountain, and Millennium Monument. It also hosts seasonal fixtures like a massive holiday tree and an ice-skating rink. Countless photo opportunities within one location!


ESTIMATED PRICE: FREE

Navy Pier | Recreation


Navy Pier is located at 600 E. Grand Avenue. The pier was built in 1916 and is one of the few structures built east of Lake Shore Drive. Although admission to some events is expensive, enjoying the views along the pier is free!
