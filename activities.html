ACTVITIES

Chicago Architecture Center River Cruise | Outdoors


View and listen to the historical background of more than fifty of the buildings along the Chicago river. The cruise lasts about an hour and a half. There is a traditional bar and snack bar on board as well. Evening cruises are also available for a few extra dollars. The cruise departs at 112 E Wacker Drive.

Chicago Gangster and Ghost Tour | Outdoors


This two-in-one walking tour spans a 1.5 mile stretch of downtown Chicago. Some of the locations you'll visit are the Congress Hotel, Death Alley, and the Palmer House. The tour lasts about two hours in total. The meeting location is the Royal Sonesta Hotel at 71 E Upper Wacker Drive. There are four tours each day at 11AM, 1PM, 4PM, and 8PM.

Chicago Aerial Tours | Outdoors


Chicago Aerial Tours offers one-of-a-kind views of the city you can't get any other way. Tours last about 20–30 minutes. There are three options: helicopter, airplane, and an airplane flight lesson with space for one passenger and student. Chicago Aerial Tours is located at the private terminal at the Midway airport and 6150 S Laramie Avenue. Payments should be made on the company's website.

360 Chicago | Observatory


The 360 observatory is located on the 94th floor of 875 North Michigan Avenue. This unique observatory contains the opportunity to experience The Tilt, which leans visitors atop panels that move to face the streets below. 360 Chicago is open from 9 AM to 11 PM on regular days. Although the Skydeck is the tallest observatory in Chicago, 360 Chicago offers a more unique experience as well as a better view of the lakefront. There is also the opportunity to enjoy beverages at an additional cost.

Chicago Cubs at Wrigely Field | Sports


Located at 1060 W Addison Street, Wrigley Field is home to the beloved Chicago Cubs baseball team. While watching a game, you can purchase traditional Chicago ballpark food starting at $10. Although parking varies from $50-$60 per game, you can consider taking the CTA elevated train instead. Tickets start around $35 from July 31st to August 3rd. Wrigely Field also offers several pre-game activities and events before each game, so make sure to arrive early!

Great Escape Room Chicago | Recreation


At 160 E Grand Avenue #400, you can find Chicago's Great Escape Room. It is both a challenging & exciting group activity. The four main escape rooms are: Escape The Colosseum: The Underworld Maze, Infirmary: Surgical Crisis, Poker Night at the President's Bunker, and Escape Artist: The Final Séance. The venue also offers two 60-minute scavenger hunts: Sherlock's Study: What's Up Watson? and Moriarty's Gameroom: The Hunt is Afoot! Each escape room holds a different quantity of people ranging from 8 to 12 players and the location is open from 4 PM to 11 PM.
